1.What does the name Doraemon mean?

You will see that Doraemon's name (ドラえもん) is written with both katakana (ドラ) and hiragana (ドラ) characters.

What do these two terms signify exactly?
The name ドラ(dora) is derived from どら猫 (dora neko), which means "stray cat."
The Japanese term for "stray" is actually のら "nora,"
like in the anime ノラガミ "Noragami," which means "stray god."
However, どら "stray" is just a small deformation of のら "nora."

The second component of his name is えもん (emon), a typical addition to old Japanese male names.



2.When does Doraemon take place?

It takes place in Japan, Tokyo in an unknown neighbourhood that does not look to be very civilized but is nonetheless active.
The majority of the episodes and chapters take place in and around this town.



3.Did Doraemon have an end?

Doraemon has never officially concluded.
All endings are fake and have been written by fans and disseminated as videos.
Original authors have never offered a conclusion.
They did consider an ending in which Doraemon returned to the future, but ultimately decided against it and continued the series.



4.Are they still making new episodes for Doraemon?

They have stopped making episodes for Doraemon but they are still producing Movies for it.
